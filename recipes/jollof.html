Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof rice is a beloved West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a blend of spices. It's often served with fried plantains or chicken and is a staple at parties and family gatherings.

Each country has its own spin on Jollof, but the Nigerian version is known for its rich, spicy flavor and vibrant red color.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of long grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 4 large tomatoes (blended)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 stock cube
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 cups chicken stock or water

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pot and sauté onions until soft.
  2. Add tomato paste and blended tomatoes, cook for 10–15 minutes.
  3. Stir in curry, thyme, bay leaf, stock cube, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour in rice and chicken stock. Mix well.
  5. Cover and cook on low heat for 20–30 minutes until rice is soft and liquid is absorbed.
  6. Stir and serve hot with fried plantains or meat.
